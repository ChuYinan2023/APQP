### How It Works in Practice

**1. RfQ & Requirements Analysis**

When your team receives an RfQ package — drawings, customer-specific requirements, material specs, regulatory references — our AI tools parse and structure the requirements automatically. The output is a structured requirements matrix with special characteristics flagged, gaps identified, and OEM-specific requirements cross-referenced.

*What changes for you:* Requirements analysis that used to take days takes hours. Nothing gets missed because a CSR was buried on page 47 of the customer-specific requirements.

**2. AI-Assisted FMEA Creation**

Our system generates an initial FMEA — fully compliant with the AIAG-VDA 7-step methodology — based on your product data, process knowledge, and historical failure mode databases. This isn't a blank template. It's a pre-populated, structured starting point that your cross-functional team reviews, challenges, and refines.

*What changes for you:* Your FMEA sessions start at 60-70% complete instead of a blank spreadsheet. Your team focuses on the hard questions — novel failure modes, judgment calls on severity and detection — instead of spending the first two hours filling in obvious entries.

**3. Dynamic APQP Linkages**

In a proper APQP system, deliverables are connected: DFMEA findings drive the special characteristics list, which drives the control plan, which drives work instructions, which must be supported by validated measurement systems. In practice, these linkages break. Someone updates the PFMEA but forgets to update the control plan. A special characteristic is on the drawing but missing from the PPAP.

Our system maintains these linkages automatically. When a PFMEA entry changes, the linked control plan entry is flagged. When a special characteristic is added, the system checks that it appears in the FMEA, the control plan, the MSA plan, and the capability study plan.

*What changes for you:* Zero linkage gaps. Your PPAP package is internally consistent before anyone reviews it. Your auditors find a clean system instead of discrepancies.

**4. Control Plan Optimization**

Control plans should reflect risk: high-risk characteristics get tighter inspection, demonstrated-capable processes get reduced frequency. In practice, control plans are often copy-pasted from previous programs with generic frequencies.

Our approach generates control plan entries directly from PFMEA risk levels and adjusts inspection frequencies based on actual process capability data when available.

*What changes for you:* Inspection effort is proportional to risk. You're not over-inspecting capable processes or under-inspecting risky ones. Cost of quality goes down while actual quality control improves.

**5. PPAP Assembly**

A PPAP package has 18 elements drawn from multiple sources — design records, FMEAs, process flow, control plan, MSA results, dimensional data, capability studies, material certifications. Assembling a complete and internally consistent package is time-consuming and error-prone.

Our system compiles PPAP elements from across your quality data, performs consistency checks, flags gaps, and produces the submission package including the PSW.

*What changes for you:* PPAP preparation time drops significantly. First-time customer approval rates go up because the package is complete and consistent.

**6. FMEA Migration (AIAG-VDA 7-Step)**

If your existing FMEAs are in the old format (RPN-based, 10-column), we can migrate them to the new AIAG-VDA 7-step format with Action Priority. This isn't just reformatting — the AI analyzes your existing content and restructures it into the proper structure tree, function net, and failure net hierarchy.

*What changes for you:* Your January 2027 compliance problem is solved systematically, not one FMEA at a time by your overloaded quality team.
