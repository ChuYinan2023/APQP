### 章节 1 General/Introduction (p.4)

#### 1.1 Purpose
This standard covers the environmental, physical, electrical, mechanical, functional, regulatory and durability requirements for Diesel Fuel Filters for Injection Engines.

#### 1.2 Component/Commodity Description
This specification can be applied to filters for systems with working pressure range from 0 to 700 [kPa] - 0 to 101.5 [psi]

#### 1.3 Application
This standard is limited in its application to those CAD files, drawings or engineering illustrations which call out this standard number or refer to this standard within some other standard or specification.

#### 1.4 Hazardous Material Control
Products and processes used to manufacture the products listed in this standard must conform to the employee and consumer health, employee safety, regulated substances, recycling and environmental reporting requirements.

#### 1.5 Mandatory, Recommended or Specified Tests
Mandatory or suggested tests if any provided within this standard are meant to assure compliance to the specification but do not relieve the responsible entity from ensuring that the component or system meets all environmental, physical, electrical, mechanical, functional, regulatory and durability requirements including all applicable system interface requirements as described in the standard for the entire duration of specified useful life.

#### 1.6 Test Planning
A test plan (DVP&R) shall be developed to verify and validate all required functions specified in this standard. The responsible entity shall provide any additional testing that's deemed necessary to meet functional requirements; the tables at the end of this document provide summary of all the requirements and shall be used to develop the test plan.

Samples utilized for Design Verification (DV) shall meet specified drawings tolerance range. Samples for Production Validation (PV) shall meet statistical capabilities for machine and manufacturing processes.

Where necessary, each line item of the test plan (DVP&R) must be supported by a comprehensive test or laboratory procedure. A test plan (DVP&R) agreement by S does not relieve the responsible entity from ensuring that the component or system meets all environmental, physical, electrical, mechanical, functional, regulatory and durability requirements including all applicable system interface requirements, in the entity's area of responsibility, as described in the standard for the specified useful life.

#### 1.7 Suggested, Recommended or Specified Components and/or Suppliers
Suggested, recommended or specified components and/or suppliers, if any, are still required to comply to the specification and do not relieve the responsible entity from ensuring that the component or system meets all environmental, physical, electrical, mechanical, functional, regulatory and durability requirements including all applicable system interface requirements, in the entity's area of responsibility, as described in the standard for the specified useful life.

#### 5.2 Resistance to Corrosion (p.10)

**Purpose:** This standard describes corrosion-related functional and appearance objectives for component.

**Procedure:**
A. Perform the test according to **CS.00251** standard whose duration is 180 cycles.
B. Test per **SAE J2334**, Immersion Method.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- After 30 cycles test check:
  - Filter integrity and no corrosion after visual inspection (according to under hood appearance) and
  - Absence of rust on metal parts.
- At the end of the 180 cycles test no loss of function filter integrity:
  - Outwards sealing,
  - Functionality of the electric heater,
  - Functionality of the NTC temperature sensor,
  - Functionality of the water in fuel sensor,
  - Residual tightening torque of threaded elements on filter and
  - Fuel fitting torque test.

#### 5.3 Resistance to Fluids in Engine Compartment (p.10-11)

**Purpose:** This test is to check mechanical and functional behavior of filter with several fluids.

Test to be performed for filter assembled in engine compartment: for different positioning, refer to the CTS.

**Procedure:**
A. Stabilize the filter (with all couplings closed) for 24 h at 80 ± 2 [°C] – 176 ± 35.6 [°F]
B. Immerse the filter in the following fluids for 1 min:
  - liquid for engine washing (5% KOH + 25% K2CO3 + 70% H2O) at 23 ± 2 [°C] – 73.4 ± 35.6 [°F];
  - ASTM 3 engine oil (Proc. Spec. **FPW9.55535** – Class D2) at 80 ± 2 [°C] - 176 ± 35.6 [°F];
  - brake fluid (Proc. Spec. **MS.90039** Class DOT 3, DOT 4) at 80 ± 2 [°C] - 176 ± 35.6 [°F];
  - engine cooling liquid (50% mono-ethylene + 50% H2O) at 80 ± 2 [°C] - 176 ± 35.6 [°F];
  - battery liquid at 50 ± 2 [°C] – 122 ± 35.6 [°F].
C. Put the filter in the oven for 22 h at 110 ± 2 [°C] – 230 ± 35.6 [°F].

**Acceptance criteria:**
At the end of the test check:
- filter integrity (visual inspection);
- functionality of the electric heater;
- functionality of the NTC temperature sensor;
- functionality of the water in fuel sensor;
- absence of red rust on metal parts;
- residual tightening torque of threaded elements on filter;
- visually that the marks on the filter can be read easily

##### 6.4.6 Resistance to Electrolysis of Water-In-Fuel Probe (p.15)
The diesel filter may contain electric probes to determine the presence of water or to indicate when the reservoir needs emptying. These probes may be subject to electrolysis with subsequent breakdown. This test shall insure the effects of electrolysis do not compromise the long term performance of the diesel filter.

**6.4.6.1 Test Procedure:**
Required Test Items: 14 volts / 2 amp DC power source; Container with tap water; Non-iodized salt.
Preparation: 1% salt by weight in normal tap water. Probe blades submerged 13mm below water surface.
Test: Adjust power supply to 14 volts. Run test for 168 hours.

**Acceptance Criteria:** After performing the test, the probe shall successfully complete Water-in-Fuel Signal Level.

##### 6.4.7 Dimensional Check of Fuel Fittings (p.15)
The fuel fittings shall be design to be leak resistant and retain the connectors.
The fuel fittings shall comply with the dimensional requirements of **SAE J2044**.

##### 6.4.8 Water-in-Fuel Signal Level (p.15-16)
Water-in-Fuel sensor shall provide a signal at the appropriate water level.
Test Materials: Unit water collection bowl with probe; Electronic signal circuit and appropriate power supply; Tap water; 100 ml Graduated cylinder- 5% accuracy.

**Completion of Test:**
1. Hook up probe to electronic signal circuit and power supply.
2. Slowly fill level water collection bowl with tap water.
3. When electronic circuit operates (lights), stop adding water. Pour water from bowl into graduated cylinder and note ml volume. Repeat once and average.

**Acceptance Criteria:** Water-in-Fuel Probe must provide a signal for the proper operation of the production electronic signal circuit when filter bowl water volume reaches (**See CTS**).

##### 6.4.9 Heater Shut Off Test (Automated Heater) (p.16-17)
Part 1: Test to determine if thermostat opens the electrical circuit in empty or full filter, before heater or unit housing is damaged.
Part 2: Test to determine if the heater fails without external damage if the thermostat fails as a closed electrical circuit.

**Test Equipment:** Electrical power supply: 14 volts at 12 amps minimum; Stopwatch; Refrigeration to -18 [°C]; Diesel fuel MS-10756.

**Acceptance Criteria:**
1. Part One: Heater turns off before 10.0 minutes when filter is full of fuel, or before 3.0 minutes when filter is empty.
2. Part Two: Heater fails (when thermostat has failed in closed position) without any signs of external damage to heater and feed through or internal damage to filter element or bowl. Heater must fail "safe" either when element is full of fuel or empty.

##### 6.4.10 Functionality of the Electric Heater (p.17-18)
Vehicle/heater interconnection circuit scheme will be provided in CTS, based on specific component and application.

**6.4.10.1 Heater General Performance requirement**
Heater shall be designed to meet minimum efficiency criteria.
Efficiency η = Q/E = (Φ/3600s · d · Cp · ΔT) / (Vbat · Iabs · 1s)

Where: Q = heat exchanged with fuel; E = electrical energy from power source; Φ = flow rate (l/h); d = fluid density; Cp = fluid specific heat; ΔT = temperature difference; Vbat = power supply voltage; Iabs = current absorbed.

Heaters shall have a minimum efficiency **η = 0.75** after 6 minutes from test beginning.

**General test parameters:**
- Test fluid: Distilled water
- Test fluid T: 14.5 [°C] – 58 [°F]
- Cp: 4180 J/KgK
- d: 1 Kg/l
- Φ: 130 l/h
- Vbat: 12.5V
- Test duration: 7 minutes
- Use 0.5 meters of original wiring harness when possible, otherwise copper wires ≥2.5 mm² cross-section.
- Data logger frequency at least 5Hz.

**6.4.10.2 Heater Specific Performances requirements (application-based)**
The CTS will contain specific, application-based further requirements on heater operation logic and performances.

**Example CTS application-based requirement:**
- Test fluid: Arctic Diesel Class 3 (acc. To EN590)
- Test fluid T: -26 [°C] - -14.8 [°F]
- Φ: 130 l/h
- Vbat: 12.5V
- Test duration: 7 minutes
- Max peak current: 40A
- Max peak duration: 2.5s (time in which current > maximum working current)
- Max working current: 30A
- ΔT minimum: 3 [°C] – 37.4 [°F] (Delta T measured after 6 minutes)

### 章节 7 Functional Requirements (p.19-33)

#### 7.1 Filtering Efficiency (p.19-20)
**Purpose:** Multi pass-type filtering test with continuous injection of contaminating agent per ISO 19438.

**Parameters:**
- Contaminating agent: must meet Standard **ISO 12103**-A3 medium grade.
- Test fluid: must meet MIL-H-5606 or AIR 3520.
- Flow rate: according to CTS.

**Procedure:**
A. Carry out the measurement according to Standard **ISO 19438**.
B. End of test at pressure drop of 250 [kPa] - 36.3 [psi].

**Table 5 - Acceptance criteria - Filtering efficiency**

| Particle dimension [μm(c)] | Initial and average efficiency according to ISO 19438 [%] |
|---|---|
| > 4 | 90 |
| > 5 | 95 |
| > 10 | 99 |
| > 15 | 99 |
| > 20 | 99.9 |
| > 30 | 99.9 |

#### 7.2 Filter Efficiency - Single Pass Particle Retention (p.20)
The HFFWS shall provide filtration protection to downstream components.
Test per **ISO 19438**.
**Acceptance Criteria:** Single pass particle retention shall be no less than 95% at 5 microns.

#### 7.3 DHC (Dust Holding Capacity) (p.20-21)
**Purpose:** Determines contaminant mass capability to define maintenance operation frequency.

**Parameters:**
- Test fluid: Diesel fuel (**EN590** or **MS-10756**)
- Contaminating agent: Carbon black + Aluminum oxide dust (M1/M2 of **ISO 12103**-2)
- Flow rate: according to CTS.
- Pressure drop at test end: according to CTS.

**Design data:**
- K = service interval (km)
- CC = fuel consumption (l/100km) according to CTS
- FM = factor 1 for good fuel market, 0.34 for bad fuel market
- AS = specific accumulation (4.8 mg/l - worst case good fuel from survey data)
- DHC = AS · (K·CC)/(100·FM)

**Procedure:** According to **ISO 4020**.

**Acceptance criteria:** Test result is positive if injected grams > DHC value with pressure drop equal to CTS limit. After test, submit to Functionality of Filter Assembly at Cold Condition (7.13).

#### 7.4 Outwards Sealing <D> (p.21)

##### 7.4.1 Sealing Test with Air
**Procedure:** Close outlet, connect inlet at max working pressure. Wait 30s. Test at -40°C, 23°C, and 80°C.
**Acceptance criteria:** Leak value < 3 cm³/min.

##### 7.4.2 Sealing Test with Fuel
**Procedure:** Close outlet, connect inlet with fuel at 10.3 bar (150 psi). Fuel is Diesel per **EN590** Artic fuel class 4. Test at -40°C, 23°C, and 80°C.
**Acceptance criteria:** No leaks are allowed.

#### 7.5 Seal of Filtering Element Gasket on Dirt/Clean Sides (p.22)
**Purpose:** Check tightness of internal sealing.
**Procedure:** Tests at -25°C, +23°C, +80°C in diesel EN590. Also at +80°C with ISO 4113. Then repeat at -25°C and 23°C after static ageing at 80°C for 800h in EN590 + 30% FAME.
**Acceptance criteria:** Maximum leak of 3 cm³/min with pressure ≥ 500 [kPa] in diesel fuel.

#### 7.6 Inner Seal Between Dirt Side/Clean Side (Bubble-Test) (p.22)
**Purpose:** Check integrity of filtering element and correct sealing.
**Procedure:** Per **ISO 4020** (method **ISO 2942**). Feed compressed air at 1.3 ± 0.1 [kPa].
**Acceptance criteria:** Air leak from outlet < 100 cm³/min.

#### 7.7 Pressure drop on new product (p.22-23)
**Parameters:**
- Test liquid: Diesel fuel (Standard **FPW.55520/01**)
- Max Δp: 30 [kPa] at 200 l/h
- Report diagram p = F(Q [l/h]) in flow rate range 10-220 l/h
**Procedure:** Per **ISO 4020**.
**Acceptance criteria:** According to CTS.

#### 7.8 Filter Cleanliness (p.23-24)
**Purpose:** Check filter clean down cleanliness.
**Parameters:** Test fluid: **ISO 4113** / Shell S5585
**Procedure:** Let ≥1000 cm³ of fluid flow through. Check particle distribution by optical method.
**Acceptance criteria:** Total weight of contamination < 0.5mg/part.

**Parameters:**

**Acceptance criteria:** Efficiency > 95%.

#### 7.13 Functionality of Filter Assembly at Cold Condition (p.25-27)
**Purpose:** Check performances during severe fuel conditions.
**Parameters:** Supply voltage: 12.5 V and 8 V. Test fluid: commercial diesel per **EN590** - grade D (min FAME 5%).

**Acceptance criteria:** Pressure drop ≤ 400 [kPa] at minimum flow rate (according to CTS).
