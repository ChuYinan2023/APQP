| 50 | - | A center-position characteristic curve (= TYPICAL characteristic curve; corresponds with the average volumetric flow rate characteristic curve between the MIN and MAX module) for the fuel supply module is stored in the ECM. This center-position characteristic curve is used as basis for actuating the fuel supply module via the pre-control characteristic map ("actuation of fuel supply module as a function of system pressure and fuel consumption"). **2.) Adapting the lowest possible fuel pressure (p.6)**

| lfd.Nr. | ID | Performance Specification Contents | Aenderungen |
|---------|----|------------------------------------|-------------|
| 51 | - | **2.) Adapting the lowest possible fuel pressure** | |
| 52 | - | Different engine series mean that different calibration strategies must be employed. | |
| 53 | - | Calibration strategy 1 ("wobbling adaption"): | |
| 54 | - | During constant driving operation, the low pressure is repeatedly decreased by increments to lower system pressure values by "wobbling" (the lower limit is 50 kPa). Depending on the fuel properties (vapor pressure), this "wobbling adaption" is used to determine the minimum system pressure at which the vehicle can be operated. The adaption to this minimum low pressure takes place by gas bubble detection in the high-pressure area and, if gas bubbles are detected, by increasing the low fuel pressure to the stable operating range. | |
| 55 | - | This means the wobbling adaption makes it possible to operate the fuel supply module at a constant pressure of approx. 100 kPa, during the adaption phase also at 50 kPa for short periods. | |
| 56 | - | Suction jet pump functioning: In order to ensure the suction jet pump is functional (e.g., swirl pot filling; secondary-chamber extraction, if applicable) at a constantly low system pressure, a function is implemented in the ECM to maintain a minimum, lower actuation limit for the fuel supply system starting from a calibratable fuel tank fill level and engine fuel consumption. This lower actuation limit is based on the design of a fuel supply system with a minimum delivery rate. It is dimensioned such that the function of the suction jet pumps is ensured throughout the entire operating temperature range. | |
| 57 | - | Due to the permanently calibrated actuation limit, there is an elevated low fuel pressure in a system above the MIN delivery rate as a result of the tolerance between MIN and MAX delivery rate. Depending on the tolerance range of the fuel supply systems, operating pressures up to the maximum possible system pressure can thus be achieved, since there is no upper actuation limit (i.e., in extreme cases up to the pressure limit of the fuel pressure regulator in the fuel filter). This means that in the worst-case scenario (e.g., permanent vehicle operation below the calibrated fuel tank fill level), a continuous increased system pressure is possible. | geaendert |
| 58 | - | Application strategy 2: | |
| 59 | - | During driving operation, the low pressure is reduced outside of dynamic driving cycles. At the same time, the high pressure is monitored with the high-pressure fuel pressure sensor. If the closed-loop control element in the high-pressure area exceeds a calibrated value due to the beginning of vapor bubble formation, the low pressure is increased until the high pressure stabilizes. In contrast to "calibration strategy 1," the lower limit of the operating pressure in the pre-control characteristic map is 300 kPa. The "wobbling strategy" for low pressures below 300 kPa is not allowed in this case. | |
| 60 | - | High-pressure monitoring: Calibration strategies 1 and 2 both include what is referred to as high-pressure monitoring. If a calibrated control deviation between high ACTUAL and high TARGET pressure occurs in the high-pressure area of the high-pressure fuel pump, the "monitoring function" of the engine-side high pressure is triggered. The purpose of this is to use a maximum actuation of the fuel supply module and associated increase in the system pressure up to the opening pressure of the fuel pressure regulator to stabilize the high pressure. If required, this full actuation can last a few minutes until a reduction in the actuation back to the previous operating state is allowed. Emergency strategy: The purpose of the function-integrated emergency strategy is a continuous maximum actuation of the fuel supply module during a driving cycle when a calibrated number of high-pressure monitoring functions is reached. The assumption is that the increased number of triggered monitoring functions occurred due to a failure in the low-pressure fuel system. This function to prevent the formation of vapor bubbles is intended to maintain driving operation. The reset to normal actuation does not take place until there is a terminal 15 off/on. This measure makes it possible to operate the fuel supply module at maximum actuation (and at the resulting maximum pressure) for an extended period (e.g., as long as it takes to empty a full tank) in the worst-case scenario. | |
